The 17½ rule — why your age matters

If you are at least 17½ years old, the DMV lets you get your instruction permit without completing Driver Education or Driver Training first. That's a real shortcut most people don't know about.

The catch: to actually get your driver's license before turning 18, you'll still need to show proof of completed Driver Ed & Training — or simply wait until your 18th birthday, when that requirement disappears entirely. Many 17½ applicants choose to wait for 18 rather than take driver's ed, since the permit itself never requires it at this age.

Your roadmap

  1. 1

    Create a MyDMV account (you + a parent/guardian)

    You and a parent or guardian each create a separate MyDMV account. They must approve your application online before your appointment.

  2. 2

    Start the DL/ID application & schedule your appointment

    Fill out the Driver License/ID application online, upload required documents (proof of identity, California residency, Social Security number), and book a DMV field office appointment.

  3. 3

    Attend your appointment with a parent/guardian

    Bring your documents. You'll give a thumbprint, pass a vision exam, get your photo taken, pay the application fee, and take the knowledge (written) test — 46 questions, need 38 correct (≈83%) to pass. You get 3 attempts with a 7-day wait between retakes.

  4. 4

    Practice — 50 supervised hours (10 at night)

    With your permit, drive only with a licensed adult who is 25 or older in the front passenger seat. Log at least 50 hours of practice, including 10 hours after dark, certified by your parent/guardian.

  5. 5

    Hold your permit / meet the driver's-ed requirement

    Under 18, you generally need 6 months holding your permit, plus completed Driver Ed & Training, before testing. At 17½+, you can skip driver ed/training on the permit — but you'll need it (or to simply turn 18) before the actual license.

  6. 6

    Pass the behind-the-wheel drive test

    Bring a registered, insured, mechanically sound vehicle and a licensed driver 25+. 3 attempts allowed, 14-day wait between retakes, $9 retest fee.

  7. 7

    Drive on a provisional license (first 12 months)

    No driving 11 p.m.–5 a.m. and no passengers under 20, unless a licensed driver 25+ rides along — with documented exceptions for school, work, or medical needs. No cell phone use at all, even hands-free.
